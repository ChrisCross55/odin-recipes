Mashed Potatoes

A Thanksgiving staple thats great year-round

Mashed Potatoes is considered a comfort food to many Americans. A Thanksgiving dinner doesn't feel complete without it. Mashed potatoes can be a very simple dish consisting of few ingredients or a complex dish involving much more, but at its core it simply consists of boiled potatoes being mashed and typically mixed with butter and milk.

Ingredients

  • Four Potatoes
  • A quarter stick of butter
  • Half a cup of milk
  • Salt
  • White Pepper
Black pepper can be used but will stand out in the food

Steps

  1. Fill a pot with enough water to cover potatoes and heat on high so it boils
  2. Wash and peel potatoes
  3. Roughly quarter potatoes
  4. Once water is boiling, add potatoes and salt into the water
  5. After fifteen minutes, check potatoes with a knife or fork for doneness. You should be able to stick the knife or fork into a chunk with relative ease. If ready, set aside a cup of the water and drain the rest
  6. Add butter and milk to the potatoes and mash
  7. Add salt and pepper to taste and if consistency is too thick, add a splash of reserved water
