Easy Crock Pot Carnitas

Mexico's answer to pulled pork

Carnitas is an extremely popular dish in Mexico. It is traditionally made by cooking tough cuts of pork in lard until it is extremely tender and the exterior is browned and caramelized. They go great in a taco topped with pickled onions or in a torta with onions and cilantro.

Ingredients

  • Pork shoulder
  • Pork lard, can substitute with chicken or beef stock
  • Can of Coca Cola
  • Orange
  • salt
  • bay leaf
Traditionally made with lard but many people replace the lard with stock in order to make it healthier.

Steps

  1. Turn on oven and set to 450 or max temp
  2. Cut pork shoulder into fist-sized chunks
  3. Roast pork in oven until browned to your liking
  4. Place pork, lard, and bay leaf in crock pot and set to low
  5. Halve orange, squeeze juice into pot, and place orange rinds in pot as well
  6. Let cook for an hour and then empty Coca Cola into pot
  7. Typically this takes 4 to 6 hours, after 3 hours add salt to your liking and start checking every half hour.
  8. Once tender to your liking, turn off crock pot and enjoy
